Analyzing Cobb 500 Growth in Malaysia

In Malaysia's commercial broiler production (e.g., closed-house ventilation cooling systems maintained at 24°C–32°C), growth dynamics determine exact profit margins. Using standards adapted from the Department of Veterinary Services (DVS) Malaysia, this model tracks a Cobb 500 broiler's weight gain over a 42-day (6-week) cycle.

In animal technology, evaluating simple bodyweight is insufficient. We need to calculate the first-order derivative \( f'(t) \), which represents the Average Daily Gain (ADG). Real-time ADG alerts farmers to physical transitions, allowing them to shift feeds (starter to grower to finisher) precisely when growth acceleration peaks.

Calculus & Logistic Model

Broiler Weight Model: \( f(t) \)

A logistic growth model accurately captures early acceleration and mature deceleration:

$$f(t) = \frac{W_{\max}}{1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)}}$$
\(W_{\max}\) = 3,100g (Target)
\(k\) = 0.16 (Rate)
\(t_0\) = 24.5d (Inflection)

First Derivative / Daily Gain Rate: \( f'(t) \)

Applying the chain and quotient rules reveals the growth rate (grams per day):

$$f'(t) = \frac{W_{\max} \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(t - t_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)})^2}$$
Alternatively derived as a function of current weight: \( f'(t) = k \cdot f(t) \cdot \left(1 - \frac{f(t)}{W_{\max}}\right) \)
